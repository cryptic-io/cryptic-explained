Making secure and encrypted online storage for everyone

Encryption solutions exist, but you, and the people you work with, don't use them. The problem is that secure and good encryption is hard. Cryptic makes encryption easy, without compromising security or privacy.

Traditional File Storages...

Traditional file storages use an encrypted connection between the server and your browser, but the files are still stored as plaintext. Meaning files can be opened, analyzed, and mined without your knowledge.

A Different Approach

Cryptic uses Client Side encryption to encrypt your files locally on your computer. The server receives your encrypted your encrypted data where it redundantly stores your files. It's impossible for Cryptic, or anyone else, to access your files without your knowledge. Cryptic makes your files accessible from anywhere, and secure from anyone.

How Cryptic works

Cryptic works similarly to PGP. Each user has a public/private key that they use to sign messages to a server and encrypt passwords to individual files. Every file is encrypted with a unique random password.

Specifically...

Your file gets split up into little chunks so that the chunks can be proccessed in parallel. It also facilitates resuming an interrupted download/upload.

Each chunk gets encrypted in parallel using CCM with a 256 bit key and AES

Encryption is done using CCM, which encrypts and authenticates data. Cryptic uses the Stanford Javascript Crypto Library.

Only the encrypted chunks are sent to the server.

The key to the files are encrypted with your login password. Only the encrypted version of the file keys are sent to the server.

Everything the server gets will always be encrypted by the client. The server has no reason to know anything except that these are binary files.

Each encrypted file is copied at least 3 times, and stored on 3 different servers. Your files aren't going anywhere.

How to use

Anonymous Upload

Anonymity is the greatest tool for privacy. You can start uploading and sharing information securely right away! (sans hoops).

Likewise, anyone you explicitly give permission to see your files can download them right away, no account needed.

Private Account

Private accounts provide all the benefits of having a managed system, without compromising privacy.

All account information exists encrypted on our servers, and is only decrypted locally on your computer. We don't even keep a hash of your password. (check out security for more info.)

Safe and Private sharing, made easy

You explicity declare who sees your files and how.

When sharing a file with someone else you can specify a normal link that will work until you delete a file, a time limited link | which will work for a specified time, and a one time only link which, you guessed it, can only be downloaded once before disappearing.

Secure Login

When one password isn't enough, we support (and encourage!) 2 factor authentication.

By using your smart phone as another means of authentication, online attacks are effectively thwarted, and physical attacks become significantly harder.

Principles

Open Source

There are two basic types of security:

  • Security through obscurity (As long as nobody notices the hole in the wall, there isn't one)
  • Security through openness (Show as many people as you can the wall. If there is a hole, let's fix it right now)

We live for open source code. We firmly believe nothing can be truly secure if it isn't open sourced. Therefore, anything that interacts with your raw data is completely open source, no exceptions (Source).

No tracking. Not now. Not ever.

Privacy has become a second class citizen, and we've come to accept a loss of privacy in exchange for simplicity. Cryptic doesn't make that compromise, and we don't allow our users to make that compromise either. Anything you do on Cryptic is known to you, and to you alone. We don't collect any personal data about our users, and we never will.

Encryption, with rounded corners

Exisiting solutions for safely encrypting and storing data are cumbersome. PGP is a wonderful tool, but it has a high barrier of entry and is impractical when sharing files with non-PGPers. Cryptic solves these problems by creating a beautiful, simple, and easy to use interface to encrypt, store, and share your files, all without having to install a single piece of hardware or software.

Damage Proof

Hard drives are delicate little things; they spin a magnetic disk at insanely fast speeds. If some natural disaster doesn't destroy your data, it's simply a matter of time before the drive itself dies. The best solution we have so far is to make it somebody else's problem, usually insecurely. Cryptic stores your encrypted data reduntantly across several drives and across different continents.
